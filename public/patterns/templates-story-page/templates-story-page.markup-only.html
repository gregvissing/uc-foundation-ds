Young alum pays it forward

UC DAAP alum creates architecture scholarship for passionate international students

2 minute read February 8, 2024 Share on facebook Share on Twitter Share on LinkedIn Print Story

In the world of college scholarships, talented international students often find themselves sifting through lists of awards and endowments, only to find they are ineligible for most financial grants without U.S. citizenship.

But that didn't stop Saakshi Terway, DAAP '17. Terway came to the University of Cincinnati from India because it's among the top five prestigious schools in the country for a Master of Architecture degree. "And especially for its internationally known cooperative education (co-op) program," notes Terway.

Terway found she faced more roadblocks as an international student, due to citizenship requirements. For the small amount of scholarships available for international students, she found the pool of applicants very competitive.

"My income as a student was pretty much limited to the two semesters of co-op I was granted as part of UC's architecture program," says Terway. "Getting a job off campus for international students on F1 visas is illegal, many scholarships have barriers for international students and the cost of tuition for out-of-state, international students can be more than double what in-state students pay."

Terway has turned these negatives into a positive pathway for others, by starting a scholarship fund for international students who face similar financial barriers.

Grateful path

As a result of creative thinking and help from her family, Terway was able to pay her tuition, secure housing with help from friends on campus, receive the Elizabeth Moesta White Sustainable Design Certification grant and take advantage of three co-op opportunities after helping to enroll UC's Master of Architecture program as a national STEM program.

"If it wasn't for my grandfather, I would not have been able to finish my master's program here," says Terway. "I started this scholarship award and named it after him as a way to continue his mission to support the right to an education."

Terway set up her scholarship so DAAP will select the international students based on their portfolio and not the GPA system.

"As an international student, the GPA system here is different, which makes it difficult for many international students to earn financial awards," says Terway. "Instead, I don't want an international student's GPA to factor in the selection, I want the passion and portfolio to be the deciding factor. If you have a strong design sense it will show up in your portfolio."
